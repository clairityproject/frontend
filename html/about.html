What is CLAIRITY? Our Mission:

Assess the exposure of the MIT population to airborne pollution through the design and implementation of a distributed air quality sensor network on MITâ€™s campus.


Air quality and well-being are directly connected, as poor air quality has proven detrimental to human health. Currently, air quality is monitored at select places throughout a city, but because of their sparse locations they do not give very much information about the air that we are breathing. However, new sensor technology can be used to provide this information at a greater resolution. We believe it is important that the average person has accessible, easy-to-understand information on air quality, and our goal is to provide the MIT community with real-time air quality updates through data from our sensors. We hope our project will be innovative in using 21st-century technologies to rapidly inform the public on environmental issues.



Our Network


The goal of our network is to measure local air quality and make it easily understandable to the public. We deployed 24 sensor nodes on the MIT campus that monitor concentrations of five types of pollutants in both indoor and outdoor locations. Data collected from the sensors is available on our website in real time, and may be graphically displayed or downloaded by individual users. The network serves as a generic testbed or prototype for future air quality networks, for deployment across a diverse array of urban and rural environments in both the developed and developing world.
