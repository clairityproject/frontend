The Team: Who are we?


We are all students from the MIT Civil and Environmental Engineering Class of 2014, and this is our senior design lab project. After several years of experience focusing in either civil or environmental work, we regrouped in our last semester to build an innovative network project, taking advantage of our diverse experiences. CLAIRITY was built by utilizing our varied technical and qualitative expertise. Students divided into teams of 3-5 students to work on the calibration, coding, communication, logistics, or hardware aspects of our project.

Calibration: The Calibration Team ran designed and implemented laboratory tests through which our sensors were calibrated against high-fidelity lab instruments. The result is a node that reports high-accuracy air quality data at a one-hundredth of the cost.

Coding: The Coding Team was responsible for writing the code to locally store the air quality data and push it to a database. In addition, they also collaborated with many of the other teams to troubleshoot and optimize the code.

Communications: The Communications Team created this website in order to display the air quality data from the sensor network. They also coordinated the public displays of the network data, seen throughout campus and featured at the MIT Museum Summer Exhibit 2014.

Logistics: The Logistics team determined the locations for each of our deployed nodes and coordinated the efforts with external stakeholders (e.g. MIT Facilities) to ensure a smooth installation process. Based on the chosen locations, we developed suitable mounting and weatherproofing schemes to ensure our nodes remained safe.

Hardware: The Hardware Team assembled and wired each node at the beginning of the term and troubleshot any malfunctioning or false-reading nodes throughout the project. Hardware also prepared and assembled the casing, wiring, and power configurations, which differ depending on the location of the node.
